Easy Buckwheat Pancakes

Vegan, sugar, gluten and dairy free, contains nuts. Buckwheat contains no gluten and is good for those who need to avoid wheat.

These dense, filling and nutritious pancakes are high in protein and fibre and are both tasty and satisfying. High in protein, essential fats and dietary fibre. Low GL.

This recipe is perfect for batch cooking and will keep up to 3 days refrigerated. Stir before use if refrigerated.

Based on 2 adults following the 7 day meal plan, you should have enough remaining from day 6 recipe.

Ingredients

200g buckwheat flour

60ml hot water

300ml almond milk

1 tablespoons of almond butter

2 capsules of Viridian Organic Maca Root powder (optional)

1 teaspoon of Viridian organic raw coconut oil

Pinch of Himalayan fine rock salt

3 tablespoons viridian organic raw coconut oil

Method

  1. Place the flour in a large bowl. Add the hot water, almond milk, a pinch of salt, almond butter, maca and 1 tsp. of coconut oil mix thoroughly.
  2. Heat the coconut oil in a large frying pan or on a griddle over a medium heat. Pour a ladleful of batter onto the pan and cook both sides until golden brown.

Mediterranean Chickpea Stew

Vegan, Sugar, Gluten, Dairy and Nut Free.

Vitamin C is a huge help when it comes to absorbing iron from plant sources. When beans (a good source of iron) are cooked with tomatoes (a good source of vitamin C), the combination can double the absorption of iron. This is easy to prepare and a delight to devour! I’ve made this several times to rave reviews. High in protein, vitamin C, dietary fibre, essential fats and protein. Low GL.

This recipe is perfect for batch cooking to store refrigerated for another day.

Ingredients

2 medium leeks

2 tablespoons viridian organic raw coconut oil

1½ teaspoons ground coriander

1 teaspoon ground cumin

1 teaspoon ground fennel seed

½ teaspoon Himalayan fine rock salt

Pinch of cayenne pepper

1 x 400g can of chopped tomatoes with their juices

1 x 400g can of chickpeas, rinsed and drained

12 Kalamata or green olives, pitted and chopped Zest of 1 lemon

1 tablespoon freshly squeezed lemon juice

Cooked millet or basmati rice, or wholewheat bread, to serve

Method

  1. Trim the leeks, slit them in half lengthwise and rinse well under running water to remove any sand or grit. Coarsely chop the leeks.
  2. Heat the oil in a large saucepan over a medium heat. Add the leeks, coriander, cumin, fennel seed, salt and cayenne pepper. Cook, stirring often, for about 5 minutes, until the leeks are soft.
  3. Add the tomatoes with their liquid, the chickpeas, olives and lemon zest and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to medium to low and cook, stirring occasionally, for about 15 minutes, until the tomato sauce is very thick. Remove from the heat and stir in the lemon juice. Serve immediately over cooked millet or rice, or with wholewheat bread.

Seedy mango and date energy bars

Vegan, sugar and dairy free, contains gluten, nuts and sesame.

These satisfying homemade energy bars were developed by my friend, work colleague, home baker and nutritional coach Eleanor Hagan. These are fantastic for adults’ and kids’ lunchboxes – just don’t let your mates know or there will be none left for you come lunchtime. High in vitamin E, zinc, dietary fibre, plant protein and essential fats. Low GL.

This recipe is perfect for batch cooking to store refrigerated for another day.

You should have enough remaining from day 4 recipe.

Ingredients

150g coconut oil plus extra for greasing

1 clove garlic

80g Medjool dates, stoned and finely chopped

80g dark tahini (or you can use a pumpkin seed spread or peanut butter)

120g rolled oats (you can also use quinoa flakes)

80g chopped hazelnuts

50g dried mango

40g pumpkin seeds

40g sesame seeds

40g sunflower seeds

20g ground flaxseeds

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 180°C/gas mark 4/350°F. Lightly grease a 20cm square tin and line the base with baking parchment. In a small saucepan set over a medium heat, melt together the coconut oil, chopped dates and tahini. Stir constantly until smooth and thick.
  2. Combine the oats, hazelnuts, mango and seeds in a large bowl. Pour in the melted coconut mixture and stir well to combine.
  3. Spoon the mixture into the prepared tin and press it down firmly and evenly with the back of a spoon. Bake for about 20 minutes, until lightly golden on top.
  4. Leave to cool, then slice into bars or squares. Eat straight away or store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 10 days.

Sweet potato & Quinoa curry

Vegan, sugar, gluten, dairy and nut free, contains soya and celery.

When making broth, I usually use organic, low-salt, gluten-free stock cubes, which you can find in your local health food shop. High in plant protein, dietary fibre, essential fats and B vitamins.

Based on 2 adults following the 7 day meal plan, you should have enough remaining from day 4 recipe.

Ingredients

2 tablespoons Viridian organic raw coconut oil

1 large red onion, finely chopped

1 clove of garlic, minced

1 teaspoon grated fresh root ginger

1 low salt, gluten-free vegetable stock cube

400ml hot water

2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and diced

1 x 400g can of chickpeas, drained and rinsed

200g quinoa

1 tablespoon mild curry powder

1 tablespoon ground cumin

80g baby spinach

Himalayan fine rock salt and freshly ground black pepper

Method

  1. Heat the coconut oil in a large pan over a low to medium heat. Add the onion, garlic and ginger and cook for 5–8 minutes, until softened.
  2. Dissolve the stock cube (if using) in 400ml of hot water in a Pyrex jug. Add the broth or water, sweet potatoes, chickpeas, quinoa, curry powder and cumin to the pan.
  3. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and cover.
  4. Simmer for 20 minutes, until the quinoa and sweet potatoes are cooked through. Add a little water if the liquid evaporates before the quinoa is fully cooked.
  5. Add the spinach and stir well. Cover again and cook just until the spinach has wilted. Add salt and pepper if desired.
